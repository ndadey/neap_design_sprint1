How this card is built

Where you stand — 2024 grade-8 mathematics average scale scores, achievement-level percentages, and the significantly-higher / not-different / significantly-lower call against the nation are NAEP's own published results and official significance determinations (NCES, 2024). Two states with the same rounded score can still get different significance calls, because each estimate carries its own standard error — which is exactly why a plain-language, significance-aware read beats eyeballing a number.

Similar states — "economically similar" is defined here by closeness in the child-poverty rate (U.S. Census ACS 2023, under age 18), the single strongest economic correlate of NAEP performance. It is one reasonable peer definition among several; a fuller model would blend poverty, English-learner share, and racial/ethnic composition. The peer-average comparison is descriptive and is not significance-tested — a fairer reference point, not a verdict.

Not in this prototype slice — each state's own trend over time, other subjects and grades, and a multi-factor peer model. In a production version these would come live from the NAEP Data Service API rather than baked-in data.

Concept prototype for the Next Gen NAEP Design Sprint — not a live NAEP product. Scores & significance: NAEP 2024 Mathematics, Grade 8 (NCES / The Nation's Report Card). Child poverty: U.S. Census Bureau, ACS 1-Year, 2023. National grade-8 math: 272 in 2024 (281 in 2019, 273 in 2022).
