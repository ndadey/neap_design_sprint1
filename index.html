Three prototypes for a more usable NAEP

A small set of concept prototypes built for the design sprint, each probing a different layer of how people access, understand, and act on NAEP data — moving from diagnosing today's friction to proposing a genuinely new kind of NAEP output. Concept demos for discussion, not live NAEP products.

The arc Map what exists → Make it findable → Make something new
01

The map

The NAEP Output Landscape

Everything NAEP publishes today — report cards, data tools, released items, microdata, special studies — grouped into seven families and mapped to the six audiences who actually use them.

Demonstrates  Where access breaks down: a who-uses-what matrix shows a couple of headline products carrying everyone while microdata and methods reach almost no one.

02

Findability

The Question-First Front Door

A single entry point where you describe what you want to know in plain language; it reads your intent and routes you to the right existing NAEP resource — silently spanning both of today's separate websites.

Demonstrates  Reorganizing around the user's question instead of NAEP's filing system. It routes — but doesn't yet create anything new.

03

A new resource

The Synthesized Answer Card

Pick a state and get a plain-language, significance-aware read on its 8th-grade math results — plus a comparison to economically similar states that NAEP doesn't offer today, built from real released data.

Demonstrates  Going past routing to generate a fair, synthesized answer — the rung where NAEP starts producing something new rather than pointing at a chart.

Open →
How they fit together. Each prototype is the next rung up. The Landscape diagnoses the problem — NAEP's outputs are organized around how they're produced, and the most powerful ones reach the fewest people. The Front Door tackles findability by letting people start from their question. The Answer Card goes furthest, turning real data into a synthesized, demographically-fair answer — which raises the sharpest question for the sprint: how far past pure description is NAEP willing to go?
Concept prototypes for the Next Gen NAEP Design Sprint — not live NAEP products. Data shown in the Answer Card is real: NAEP 2024 Mathematics, Grade 8 (NCES / The Nation's Report Card) and child poverty from the U.S. Census Bureau, ACS 1-Year, 2023. Built June 2026.
