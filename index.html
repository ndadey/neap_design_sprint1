Three prototypes for a more usable NAEP

A small set of concept prototypes built for the design sprint, each probing a different layer of how people access, understand, and act on NAEP data.

01

The map

The NAEP Output Landscape

Everything NAEP publishes today — report cards, data tools, released items, microdata, special studies — grouped into seven families and mapped to the six audiences who actually use them.

Demonstrates  Where access breaks down: a who-uses-what matrix shows a couple of headline products carrying everyone while microdata and methods reach almost no one.

02

Findability

The Question-First Front Door

A single entry point where you describe what you want to know in plain language; it reads your intent and routes you to the right existing NAEP resource — silently spanning both of today's separate websites.

Demonstrates  Reorganizing around the user's question instead of NAEP's filing system. It routes — but doesn't yet create anything new.

Open →
03

A new resource

The Synthesized Answer Card

Pick a state and get a plain-language, significance-aware read on its 8th-grade math results — plus a comparison to economically similar states that NAEP doesn't offer today, built from real released data.

Demonstrates  Going past routing to generate a fair, synthesized answer — the rung where NAEP starts producing something new rather than pointing at a chart.

Open →
04

Two decisions

The State Leader Console

A console built around two decisions a state leader actually faces: whether the state’s own “proficient” bar is calibrated honestly against the NAEP scale, and where to aim initiatives and funding — fusing standing, trajectory, and equity into one priority per subject and grade.

Demonstrates  Real 2024 national NAEP anchors paired with the NCES standard-mapping method and a no-black-box, significance-tested triage — every figure tagged by its source. State values are illustrative.

How they fit together. The Landscape diagnoses the problem — NAEP's outputs are organized around how they're produced, and the most powerful ones reach the fewest people. The Front Door tackles findability by letting people start from their question. The Answer Card turns real data into a synthesized, demographically-fair answer for the public; the State Leader Console reframes NAEP around the decisions a state actually faces — is our “proficient” bar honest, and where do we aim funding — on a shared, source-tagged footing. Both raise the sharpest question for the sprint: how far past pure description is NAEP willing to go?
Concept prototypes for the Next Gen NAEP Design Sprint — not live NAEP products. The Answer Card uses real data: NAEP 2024 Mathematics & Reading, Grade 8 (NCES / The Nation's Report Card) and child poverty from the U.S. Census Bureau, ACS 1-Year, 2023. The State Leader Console pairs real 2024 national NAEP anchors with illustrative state values. Built June 2026.
